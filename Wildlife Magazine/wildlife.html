The Reindeer

The Reindeer can measure up to 260 cm long, 180 cm high and weigh up to 260kg.

The deer lives in large dense forests, clearings, meadows. It can live in the mountains up to an altitude of 2000 m.

The deer eats buds, young shoots, grasses, leaves, ferns, fruits, bramble leaves, bark, certain agricultural products.

The size of the antlers depends on the age of the animals, they fall and grow back every year.

The Elephant

The African elephant, which can reach 7 tons, is the largest living land animal. The elephant appears in many cultures. It is a symbol of wisdom in Asian culture, known for its memory and intelligence.

The tusks are formed by an extension of the upper second incisors. They serve as a tool, a weapon of defense and a sexual attribute

The elephant feeds on grass, leaves, bushes and sometimes even fruit. It absorbs daily about 150 to 280 kg of food depending on the season.

The Brown Bear

The brown bear is a species of bear that can reach a body mass of 130 to 700 kg.

Standing, this bear reaches a height of 1.5-3.5 meters. Despite their size, they can run at speeds of up to 55 km/h.

It is common for two males to fight for a female or the appropriation of a territory. However, if the winner is assured of being able to fertilize the bear, this partner does not remain faithful to him and raises the cubs alone.

The Penguin

The penguin is a seabird that lives in the North Atlantic, on the coasts of North America and Europe.

The wings of penguins, which have become unusable for flight, are on the other hand very suitable for swimming and diving.

penguins can swim up to 35 km/h (compared to 9 km/h for the best Olympic swimmer) and emperor penguins can dive more than 520 m to search for food, which is the absolute record among all birds.

The Sloth

Sloths are tree-dwelling mammals from South and Central America that make up the suborder Folivora1. They are medium-sized animals with a quirky way of life: they almost always hang upside down in trees and move slowly. They wield long claws.

The two-toed sloth eats mainly leaves, but also young shoots, small branches, buds, flowers, fruits and tuberous roots.It also feeds on insects and sometimes even small animals, such as chicks.

The Monkey

Like many primates, baboons live in organized flocks. The baboon only feels safe inside this band, dominated by a few powerful males who defend it; they are also responsible for making peace during internal quarrels.

They roam the arid savannahs and rocky places in Arabia, sub-Saharan Africa as well as the tall forests of West Africa. They feed on plants, small animals, birds and eggs.

Depending on the species, baboons weigh from 14 to 40 kg and measure between 50 and 115 cm. Females are twice as small as males.

Its forms of communication are varied: transmission of signals by means of the tail, posture, cries and yelps.
